RAG model: 

+ works given the spread of different input types (chunking) 

+ vector databases prefer semantic content rather than structured specific content. 

+ Ability to maintain extensibility to ingest reports and documents in the future. 

+ Minimal learning over time. Can learn ONLY IF a lot of people query it and grade its responses. 


Further I think a tool that can truly identify gaps in the market for us to address must be able to:

State what is mentioned in the documents we upload but also understand what it means in context with other parts of the document too. This requires more than reasoning after selection. The sort of reasoning that fine tuning makes language models capable of. H


Fine tuned language model:

- Harder to ensure that figures reported are exact (hallucinations). 

- Harder to trace where exactly the model picked up this information from. 

- Will be much better at imposing some reasoning over the dataset though. This reasoning will require a lot of training examples. 

These figures we want to extract directly from the documents themselves and be able to trace where we are getting the information from. A sort of functionality that is only achieved by rag models. But rag models do not make it easy to reason over structured data like what we have. Due to the factors I mentioned above. 

 

This complicates things a bit.
